## 3. Justification (How to defend your results to others)

If a professor, reviewer, or colleague questions the validity of your data, you can defend your pipeline using these three core architectural pillars:

1. **Local Contrast Maximization (No Hard Thresholds):** 
   You can argue that CraterX does not rely on a brittle "global threshold" to find shadows. It compares the shadowed region to its *immediate local background*. This means your algorithm works equally perfectly on bright ejecta blankets and dark mare basalt without user intervention.
2. **Sub-Pixel Precision:** 
   Your pipeline does not stop at whole pixels. The refinement algorithm iterates in 0.5-pixel steps, calculating a directional contrast score between the sunlit rim and the shadow. It mathematically finds the "sweet spot," yielding diameter measurements that are more precise than the camera's native pixel grid.
3. **Morphology-Aware Refinement:** 
   Your template-matching is not blind. Because of the fixes we made, CraterX refines the boundaries of "Sharp" craters using a dynamic template built *only* from other Sharp craters, preventing blurred measurements.
